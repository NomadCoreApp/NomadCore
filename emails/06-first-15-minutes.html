You have 15 minutes.
What do you do?

In emergencies, most people freeze for 10+ seconds. It's called normalcy bias — your brain insists "this can't be happening." The people who act fastest aren't braver. They've rehearsed.

This week's guide is a universal action framework — a single sequence that works for any emergency:

Minutes 0-1 — Stop. Assess. Three questions: Am I safe? Anyone injured? Stay or go?
Minutes 1-3 — Protect life. Get to safety. Account for family. First aid.
Minutes 3-5 — Communicate. Text (don't call). Contact your relay person.
Minutes 5-10 — Stabilize. Grab go-bag. Secure utilities. Meds. Shoes.
Minutes 10-15 — Document. Photos of damage. Check neighbors. Monitor updates.

The guide also includes a scenario quick-reference covering fire, earthquake, tornado, flood, active threat, power outage, and chemical spill — with the specific immediate action for each.
