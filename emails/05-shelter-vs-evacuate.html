Stay or go?
The decision that changes everything.

During Hurricane Katrina, people stayed when they should have evacuated. During the Texas winter storm Uri, people fled on icy roads when sheltering at home was safer.

Both decisions killed people. The difference between the right call and the wrong one isn't courage — it's a framework for deciding.

This week's guide gives you a clear decision matrix:

EVACUATE — Wildfire, flooding, mandatory order, structural damage, gas leak
SHELTER — Chemical spill, tornado warning, extreme cold, active threat nearby
GRAY ZONE — Approaching hurricane, civil unrest, prolonged power outage

The full guide covers 13 threat types with specific recommended actions, plus step-by-step instructions for how to shelter and how to evacuate properly.
