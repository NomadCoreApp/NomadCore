How to talk to your kids about emergencies

Here's what the research says: the problem isn't talking to kids about emergencies. It's not talking to them. Kids fill silence with imagination — and what they imagine is usually worse than reality.

This week's guide is our most shareable one yet. It breaks down exactly how to have these conversations at every age:

Ages 2-5: Use stuffed animals, focus on helpers, practice as games
Ages 6-9: Build competence with roles — "You're in charge of the pet carrier"
Ages 10-13: Real responsibility — first aid, reading alerts, using supplies
Ages 14+: Near-adult partnership — CPR cert, executing the plan solo

It also covers what not to say, how to run family drills that don't feel scary, and signs a child might be anxious.
