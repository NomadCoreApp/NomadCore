The First 15 Minutes

A universal action framework for any emergency.

Minutes 0–1: Stop & Assess

  • Am I safe right now?
  • Is anyone injured?
  • Do we stay or go?
  • Identify the threat type

Minutes 1–3: Protect Life

  • Move to safety (away from threat)
  • Account for all family members
  • Administer first aid if needed
  • Protect airway (smoke, dust, chemicals)

Minutes 3–5: Communicate

  • Text (don’t call) — “I’m OK” or “Need help”
  • Contact out-of-area relay person
  • Check on children / elderly / pets
  • Monitor emergency alerts (radio, phone)

Minutes 5–10: Stabilize

  • Grab go-bag if evacuating
  • Shut off gas / water / electricity if needed
  • Take medications, shoes, keys, phone charger
  • Lock up if leaving; open garage for access if staying

Minutes 10–15: Document & Help

  • Photograph damage (insurance)
  • Check on neighbors
  • Monitor official channels for updates
  • Begin longer-term plan (shelter, supplies, routes)

Scenario Quick-Reference

  • Fire: GET OUT. Low to ground. Don’t open hot doors.
  • Earthquake: DROP, COVER, HOLD. Stay indoors until shaking stops.
  • Tornado: Interior room, lowest floor. Cover your head.
  • Flood: Move to higher ground. Never drive through water.
  • Active threat: RUN → HIDE → FIGHT (last resort)
  • Power outage: Check breakers. Conserve phone battery. Open fridge minimally.
  • Chemical spill: Seal room. Wet towel under door. Wait for all-clear.
